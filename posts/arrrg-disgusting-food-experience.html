Arrrg!

The soft chatter and grumbling chairs shielded us from the whistling wind outside. We laid down all our scarves, jackets, and whatever strength we had left. After a long day of hiking, we finally retired to Pepper House.

The old, worn-out, and somewhat sticky menu rested on a table just as sticky. The adults talked loudly over each other as I split my chopsticks. I overheard something about a stinky tofu, but I paid it no mind. When they asked what I would like, I pointed to the blue mapo tofu cubes. It is usually red, but the page was so discolored that only the number of red peppers (spice level) was legible to me. They always joked, “The worse the menu, the better the food.”

We compiled our lists and placed all our menus on the spinning Lazy Susan. Our tummies’ wishes were then sent to the genies in the kitchen.

Luckily, instead of three wishes, it was nine, each filled to the brim with crackling and sizzling magic. Everyone lunged toward the Lazy Susan like a game of Hungry Hungry Hippo. I stocked my boat full of grain and meat, tending carefully to the loot within. I spun my noodle sails against the winds of conversation when a simple but daunting golden nugget was plopped onto my plate.

The red, slimy mapo tofu stared down its new crispy, neat stinky tofu counterpart. I thought, “They used to call me the garbage disposal because I eat anything. How bad could it be?” Lightly fried skin crinkled against the bamboo as I popped it in my mouth. I began to chew.

An old manuscript in a bottle washed ashore. I popped the cork, eager to see what treasures awaited. To my disdain, instead of finding pirates' booty, I dug up a pirate’s booty as it smelled heavily of rear. The dust and dank inside the bottle aged like fine milk. The taste was a fart preserved in a used rag: damp, lukewarm, and impossible to escape. The pungent fume bleached my runny nose clean.

My tongue cried mutiny as the cunning Goldbeard invaded his ship. He fought as hard as he could, pushing back the enemy until he fell overboard into a napkin. However, the damage done to the ship was irreparable. Floral tea continued to bring his ship down, and soon it was gone.

All the adults started laughing, mentioning that the enjoyment of the dish comes with age. The bottle sealed itself and continued to drift for another victim. I sat back quietly, learning that strength isn’t always about trying everything, but knowing when to let the glass keep spinning.
