Something to Chew on: A Dubai Chewy Cookie Recipe

After weeks of TikToks from my girlfriend about Dubai cookies, I decided to get her some for when she visits. It is basically a big marshmallow shell filled with pistachio cream and kataifi. With my limited recipe repertoire, especially on the dessert side, I was very intimidated.

A quick Google search showed how viral they'd become; apparently, even Korean news segments were covering how people were overspending on them. They weren't available in any stores in Baltimore yet, so I decided to make them myself and ordered all the ingredients from Amazon.

By the time she arrived, we had already spent the day moving, shopping, driving, walking, and eating until I felt utterly exhausted. When it came time to actually make the chewy cookies, I wasn't excited anymore. I was tired.

We started anyway. The kataifi needed constant attention, the pistachio filling had to be just right, and the marshmallow coating was messier than I expected. At some point, while the kataifi was toasting, I sat back in my stool. I told myself I just needed a second, but really, I was checking out.

She noticed, and we argued. Nothing huge, but enough to throw us off. She thought I had stopped helping. I thought I had just slowed down from exhaustion. The whole point of the cookies had been to do something nice for her, and suddenly it felt like I had burdened her.

But we still finished them.

They didn't look like the TikToks. More like lumpy, slightly misshapen blobs than the perfect glossy domes I had imagined. We dusted them with cocoa powder anyway and put them on a plate.

Then we tried them. They were really good: sweet, crunchy, soft, and pistachio-y. And somewhere between the first bite and the second, the tension faded. We laughed about how they looked. The argument didn't feel as important anymore.

I think that's the part I didn't expect. Not that the recipe would work, but that finishing it would matter more than how well I executed it. Effort doesn't always look like enthusiasm. Sometimes it looks like pushing through when you're tired, messing up a little, and still ending up with something shared.

Recipe: Dubai Chewy Cookies

  • Marshmallows
  • Kataifi
  • Pistachio cream
  • Butter
  • Cocoa powder

Instructions:

  1. Toast kataifi in butter over medium heat until golden and crispy. Let cool in the fridge.
  2. Mix toasted kataifi with pistachio cream to form the filling.
  3. Melt marshmallows gently (in the microwave or on the stovetop) until soft and pliable.
  4. Mix cocoa powder in with the marshmallows to give it a chocolate color.
  5. Flatten a portion of marshmallow, place filling inside, and wrap to form a ball.
  6. Dust with cocoa powder and let it set before serving.
